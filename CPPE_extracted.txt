03. Challenges Identified
Key Obstacles in CI/CD Pipelines
Four critical challenges that disrupt deployment workflows and increase recovery time
01 Configuration Errors
CI/CD pipelines frequently fail due to incorrect Kubernetes 
configurations, including improper resource limits, misconfigured 
environment variables, or syntax errors in deployment manifests.
Impact: Deployment rejection, pod instability, repeated 
pipeline failures
02 Dependency Mismatches
Build and runtime failures commonly arise from missing 
dependencies, incompatible library versions, or unverified third-party 
updates. Identifying the specific dependency requires manual log 
analysis.
Impact: Build interruptions, delayed deployments, increased 
debugging effort
03 Infrastructure Constraints
Insufficient CPU, memory, or storage allocation can lead to container 
crashes and unsuccessful deployments. Kubernetes pods may 
terminate with OOMKilled errors when memory limits are incorrectly 
defined.
Impact: Runtime instability, service downtime, extended 
recovery time
04 Manual Troubleshooting Overhead
Traditional DevOps workflows rely heavily on manual inspection of 
CI/CD logs, Kubernetes events, and system metrics. This process is 
repetitive, time-consuming, and prone to human error in distributed 
systems.
Impact: Increased MTTR, reduced developer productivity
